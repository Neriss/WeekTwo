Your Teachers

Davy Bisaro

Owner, Damask Studios / Yoga, Pilates, Fitness, Personal Training

Davy began her yoga practice and dance career in 2002 when she was living in Oxford, England. She started teaching Yoga (AFPA, 200Hr YA) and Pilates (PIA) in Chicago, IL in 2009.

Davy earned her degree in dance at SFU in 2004-08. Davy has choreographed and performed in diverse spaces, creating work for both trained and untrained movers. Davy has also collaborated with artists in a number of different mediums, including sculpture, textile, video & new media.

Davy taught and danced/choreographed in Chicago for 2 years when she relocated to Toronto, ON. She began teaching and dancing/choreographing in Toronto when she opened Damask Studios in July, 2013.

She naturally progressed into personal training in 2011. Keeping her love and continuous study of anatomy alive.

Her varied training and professional experience in movement based practices (Dance, Pilates and Yoga) has been an asset when teaching and inspired Bisaro to create Iron Pilates. This background was also the catalyst for Davy to create a studio that offers a variety of classes that challenges people’s bodies in different ways.

‘I am always exploring new ways of moving, to keep the body and mind healthy and strong. Never stop moving!’

Norah Sadava

Yoga, Pilates + Personal Training

Norah took her first yoga class at the age of 15 and has been dedicated to the practice ever since.

A performer, musician, and traveller, she has a sense of play and humor that she feels is an importantpart of any yoga practice.

Norah completed her ashtanga-based 200hr teacher training with Michael Gannon in Scotland in 2009 and, and went Norahon to complete her hatha-based 500hr training in India in 2013.

With a firm grounding in both traditions, Norah cultivates a practice that emphasizes stability and breath, as well as flow and strength.

In a fast-moving, high-pressure world, Norah underlines the importance of a consistent physical practice to ground us in our bodies, our breath, and to foster a state of ease in both mind and body.

She is supremely grateful to all of her teachers past and present, and is humbled by the endless learning process which is yoga.

Beccy Diederichs

Yoga (substitute)

I started practicing yoga almost 20 years ago with Kaila Kukla. Her two-hour flow is influenced by Iyengar, Kundalini, and Tantra yoga practices. In 2000, I completed her eight-month yoga teacher training program and began to teach classes in September 2001. My classes derive from my studies with Kaila and provide a smooth flow of postures, breath, and meditation for beginners to experienced practitioners. I am a visual artist, writer, writing consultant and tutor, and balance my yoga with pick-up ice hockey.

Namaste,
Beccy Diederichs
